Degrees & Degree Requirements

The Department of Computer Science offers a CS Minor and a CS Major with two different concentrations: Computer Systems and Information Systems.

The Computer Science Major

Both the Computer Systems and Information Systems concentrations draw from a common core of computer science courses but differ in emphasis. Both concentrations provide the student with preparation for a career in the computer field or the background necessary for studies at the graduate level.

Computer Systems Concentration

The Computer Systems concentration covers hardware and software design and prepares students for careers in scientific and engineering applications, systems programming, computer architecture design and software development.

41 credit hours

  • 13 CSCI core courses
  • 2 CSCI electives at the 300-level
  • Calculus I
  • Discrete Math
  • Physics I
  • Physics II
  • 2-3 additional math courses

See the UNCA Catalog for more information.

Information Systems Concentration

The Information Systems concentration focuses on the theory and application of software development, with emphasis on data processing applications, and prepares students for careers in commercial programming, systems analysis and design, and database management.

38 credit hours

  • 12 CSCI core courses
  • 2 CSCI electives at the 300-level
  • Calculus I
  • Discrete Math
  • Statistics

See the UNCA Catalog for more information.

The Computer Science Minor

The CSCI Minor consists of 21 credit hours. Specifically, students must successfully complete:

  • 4 required courses from the CSCI core: CSCI 18X CSCI 201 CSCI 202 CSCI 235
  • 3 CSCI electives at the 300-level

See the UNCA Catalog for more information.
